Kingslayer

Role: Lead Designer/Developer, Audio Design, Animator

Collaborators: Sirius Li (Character Design), Jonathan Newman (BGM)

Duration: 3-4 weeks (March 2020 - April 2020)

Tools Used:
  • Processing (Development)
  • Piskel.io (Pixel Art & Animation)
  • Audacity (Sound Design)

Project Description

Kingslayer is a platformer bullet hell game made as part of the class IAT 167: Digital Games: Genre, Structure, Programming and Play at SFU.

In Kingslayer, the player is pit against several bosses in their own levels, where they have to dodge against the bosses’ onslaught of different projectile attacks wielding only a sword and a pistol.

Through this project, I aimed to explore how to design fast-paced gameplay by taking inspiration from existing games in the platform bullet hell genre and making my own.

Features

Kingslayer features 3 incremental bosses with their own individual attack patterns, which increases in difficulty and complexity as the boss’s health diminishes, creating high-action gameplay that keeps players on their toes.

Kingslayer also includes a shop that allows players to upgrade their abilities after every boss, and difficulty options that allow players to play at the level they’re comfortable with.

Planning and Proposal

When making the proposal for the project, I wanted to streamline the process so that I could clearly follow up on my concept down the line. Clearly sectioning systems and different aspects of design allowed me to easily refer back to the document later.

Art and Animation

The main goal of the art and animation in the game is to clearly convey what situation the player is in, with art representing the narrative and the animation representing the state of the game.

Sound Design

In designing sound effects for the game, I recorded basic sounds with objects I had lying around and used Audacity to perform some simple edits. The main goal was to give the player a clear indication as to what was happening in the game not only through visuals, but also through sound.
